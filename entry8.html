Body horror

Body horror is a horror subgenre that focuses on the grotesque, disturbing transformation or degradation of the human body, often portraying bodily mutations, parasites, or involuntary changes. This genre taps into deep-seated fears of physical vulnerability, challenging viewers’ comfort with their own bodies by exposing the horror of physical corruption and loss of control.

The Fly (1986)


  • Director: David Cronenberg/li>
  • Cast: KJeff Goldblum, Geena Davis

A scientist, Seth Brundle, develops a teleportation device, but during an experiment, a fly accidentally enters the pod with him. Over time, he undergoes a horrific transformation, merging with the fly on a cellular level, experiencing both mental and physical degeneration.

The Fly is iconic for its horrifyingly realistic portrayal of bodily transformation, combining emotional tragedy with disturbing visuals. Cronenberg’s direction and Goldblum’s performance make it a masterpiece of body horror, examining both physical decay and psychological horror.
