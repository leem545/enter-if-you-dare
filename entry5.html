Gore

Gore films focus on excessive blood and graphic violence to create fear. They emphasize body mutilation and visceral imagery to evoke strong discomfort and tension in the audience.

Saw (2004)


  • Director: James Wan
  • Cast: Cary Elwes, Leigh Whannell,
    Danny Glover, Monica Potter

Two men find themselves chained in a strange bathroom, forced to make a gruesome choice to sacrifice the other in order to escape. Behind their ordeal is a mysterious figure known as "Jigsaw," who tests the limits of human survival instinct.

Saw was more than just a gore film. Beyond the explicit violence and blood, it highlighted the most desperate moments of human nature. The twist ending was shocking, and from the very first scene, I was utterly captivated, holding my breath throughout. Honestly, I'm not usually a fan of gore films, but this one really drew me in.
